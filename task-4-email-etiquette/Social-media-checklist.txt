Responsible Social Media: The Do's

1.Keep your profiles professional — use your real name and a clear profile photo; employers and professors look you up

2.Think before you post — ask yourself "Would I be comfortable if my institution or future employer saw this?"

3.Fact-check before sharing — verify information from at least two credible sources before resharing

4.Use privacy settings wisely — restrict personal content, photos, and check-ins to trusted connections only

5.Credit original creators — tag or mention the source whenever you share someone else's work or ideas

6.Engage constructively — comment with evidence and courtesy, even when you disagree with someone

7.Showcase your skills — share your projects, GitHub, certifications, and academic achievements to build a positive footprint


Responsible Social Media: The Don'ts

1.Don't post personal details publicly — never share your address, phone number, daily routine, or live location

2.Don't cyberbully or harass — mocking, targeting, or publicly shaming others can have serious disciplinary and legal consequences

3.Don't share confidential material — exam papers, internal college notices, or restricted content must never be posted online

4.Don't post in anger — venting publicly about professors, classmates, or your institution causes lasting damage to relationships and reputation

5.Don't spread fake news — never reshare unverified headlines or sensational claims, even if they seem believable

6.Don't plagiarise on social media — always attribute ideas, quotes, and creative work to their original authors

7.Don't click suspicious links — DMs and comments on social platforms are common phishing attack vectors; stay alert
