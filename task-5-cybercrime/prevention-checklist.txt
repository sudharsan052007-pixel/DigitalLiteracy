General Online Safety

 Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, or password with anyone — not even someone claiming to be from your bank, Google Pay, or a government office. Legitimate organisations will never ask for these.
 Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all important accounts — email, social media, college portals, and banking apps. Use an authenticator app (like Google Authenticator) rather than SMS where possible.
 Verify before you click — hover over links before opening them. If a link in an email or WhatsApp says "sbi.co.in" but the actual URL is "sbi-helpdesk.net", it is a phishing site. Do not proceed.
 Download apps only from official stores — use the Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Never install APK files sent via WhatsApp or links, even from people you know.
 Do not use public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions — avoid logging into banking apps, UPI apps, or college portals on café or campus open Wi-Fi networks. Use mobile data or a VPN instead.
 Regularly review app permissions — periodically check which apps have access to your camera, microphone, contacts, and location. Revoke permissions that are not necessary.
 Lock your phone with a strong PIN or biometric — if your device is stolen with an unlocked UPI app open, your money is accessible. Always enable screen lock and app-level locks on payment apps.
 Log out of shared devices — never save passwords on college lab computers or a friend's device. Always sign out after use and clear the browser session.


Financial & UPI-Specific Safety

 Remember: You NEVER need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. A PIN is only required to send money or authorise a debit. If anyone — buyer, HR recruiter, or "bank executive" — asks you to enter your PIN to "receive" a payment, it is fraud. Disconnect immediately.
 Do not scan QR codes sent by strangers. Fraudsters send QR codes that initiate payment requests, not receipts. Before scanning any QR code in a transaction, verify its source carefully. Prefer typing the UPI ID manually.
 Set a daily UPI transaction limit — most UPI apps allow you to set a maximum daily spend. Capping it at ₹5,000–₹10,000 limits your exposure if your account is ever compromised.
 Check your bank statement weekly — review all transactions regularly on your banking app. Report any unrecognised debit within 3 days; quick reporting significantly improves the chance of recovery.


Social Media & Identity Safety

 Do not post photos of your ID cards, marksheets, or Aadhaar online — not even temporarily in college groups. Screenshots persist. Identity documents can be misused for SIM swapping or loan fraud.
 Be sceptical of "job offers" and "scholarship schemes" on Instagram or Telegram — if it requires you to pay a registration fee or share your bank details upfront, it is almost certainly a scam.
 Report and block cyberbullying immediately — do not respond or engage. Take screenshots as evidence, block the account, and report it within the platform. Preserve all evidence before blocking.
